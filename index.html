Hello! I'm Emanuele, an AI researcher with a background in engineering physics and a current focus on the intersection of machine learning and neuroscience. I'm enrolled in the MSc Neuro-AI program at EPFL, where my research has spanned self-supervised and foundation model pipelines for EEG signals, biologically plausible speech synthesis with spiking neural networks, and neuroimaging analysis.

I tend to gravitate toward problems where the biology is not just a motivation but a genuine constraint — where taking the physiology seriously changes what the model should look like.

Current Work

Logitech Europe SA — CTO Research Office AI Research Intern · Lausanne

Building spatially invariant foundation models for EEG. The central problem is constructing representations that are robust to the spatial and configurational variability of electrode placement — permutation, dropout, and montage shifts — through contrastive learning, masked modeling, and autoencoding objectives with geometry-aware and conductivity-aware priors.

Idiap Research Institute AI Research Intern · Martigny

Developed a bio-inspired text-to-speech system by replacing the FastSpeech 2 decoder with AdLIF-based spiking neural network modules. Studied biologically plausible speech synthesis and the effect of SNN decoder dimensionality on generated speech quality.

Selected Projects

EEG Foundation Models

Spatially invariant self-supervised learning for EEG with contrastive, masked, and autoencoding objectives. Geometry-aware and conductivity-aware architectural priors for robustness to sensor configuration variability in real-world recordings.

Self-supervised learning · EEG · Geometric priors · Contrastive learning

Bio-inspired TTS — AdLIF Spiking Decoder

FastSpeech 2 with an AdLIF-based spiking decoder for biologically plausible speech synthesis. Investigation of dimensionality effects and the properties of SNN-generated speech at the architecture level.

Spiking neural networks · AdLIF · Text-to-speech · Biological plausibility

fMRI Auditory Analysis

Analysis of fMRI responses to emotional auditory stimuli: preprocessing, general linear modeling, dimensionality reduction (PCA, ICA), and unsupervised clustering (K-means) of activation patterns.

Neuroimaging · GLM · PCA · ICA · K-means

TinyLLMs for Hate Speech Classification

Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT/LoRA) of small language models for hate speech classification, with explainability analysis comparing adaptation strategies.

PEFT · LoRA · Explainability · NLP

LLM Educational Assistant

Full fine-tuning pipeline for an educational assistant: supervised fine-tuning, DPO, retrieval-augmented generation, quantization, and reasoning-oriented prompting strategies.

SFT · DPO · RAG · Quantization
